OUR VISION

We imagine a world where girls are healthy, educated, and in control of their own destiny - free from violence, disease, and limitations based on gender.

OUR MISSION

WISER works with girls to transcend poverty, HIV/AIDS, and gender-based violence. We do this by creating environments that empower young women to drive change in their communities.

OUR VALUES

EVIDENCE-BASED

WISER was founded because of the creativity and tenacity of its local community, who believed that educating their girls would change their community. The local community of Muhuru Bay continues to drive innovation and growth at WISER.

GIRL-LED

Our students are at the heart of what we do. WISER girls are fierce advocates for their own education and wellbeing, and that of their community. WISER continuously responds and adapts to our students’ needs for the present and dreams for the future.

HOLISTIC

WISER knows that a girl can’t get an education if she is hungry, or is sick, or is worried about meeting her basic needs. That’s why WISER provides students everything they need to thrive, from balanced meals to accessible healthcare to psychosocial support.

OUR STORY

In rural Kenya, education and health are intertwined for girls. Girls usually drop out of school due to pregnancy, child marriage, lacking sanitary pads, physical, sexual, and psychological abuse, or becoming AIDS orphans. Half of sexually active girls in the community have sex for money to pay for school fees, sanitary pads, or soap. This elevates their HIV risk and damages their mental health. The few girls who do complete secondary school often do so with low grades and cannot go to a university. Since our inception in 2007, WISER has provided the hardest-to-reach girls with the resources, healthcare, and education they need to overcome poverty and gender inequity. WISER now serves 240 girls who are change-makers for their families and community, achieving unprecedented outcomes and laying foundations for ever-brighter futures.
